All randomness flows through a **single seeded PRNG** (mulberry32 seeded from `state.seed`) so a game is fully replayable and **server-verifiable** (anti-cheat for the leaderboard). No `Math.random()` anywhere in the engine.\n- **FastAPI** adds three thin endpoints (new router `app/routes/api_game.py`), reusing existing `get_db`, models, and `settings`:\n  - `GET /api/game/seed` \u2014 picks a real trial, returns the **GameSeed** (Section 9).\n  - `POST /api/game/review` \u2014 the optional LLM \"FDA Reviewer\" (Section 7). Pure scripted fallback if `settings.openai_api_key` is empty or the call fails.\n  - `POST /api/game/score` \u2014 re-runs the action log through a server copy of the engine, validates the final score, writes to a `game_scores` leaderboard table (email + display name, no auth).\n- **Wiring discipline (per CLAUDE.md):** the engine is the single source of truth; the UI only dispatches actions and renders `state`. The score endpoint *replays* the log rather than trusting a client-sent number \u2014 fail loud if replay \u2260 claimed score.\n\n---\n\n## 1. Theme & Goal\n\nYou are the **regulatory strategist** for a startup shepherding **one real candidate product** (drug, biologic, or device \u2014 taken verbatim from the seeded trial's lead intervention) from its current stage through to **FDA market authorization**, before:\n\n- your **Capital** hits $0 (\"You are out of money!\" \u2014 the original board's lose screen), **or**\n- the **Competitor** reaches approval first (\"Scooped \u2014 they got the label claim you wanted\"), **or**\n- you run out of the **Quarters** allotted (patent cliff / investor patience expires).\n\n**Win** = receive FDA **Approval/Clearance** with resources to spare. Score rewards *speed + efficiency + evidence quality*; it punishes wasted capital, sloppy submissions, and goodwill burned on shortcuts.\n\nThe 6 original characters become **selectable \"advisor\" personas** (a light strategic modifier + the comedy), see Section 4.6.\n\n---\n\n## 2. Resources (the state variables)\n\nFour resources. Two are *survival* (lose at zero), two are *quality* (gate outcomes and score).\n\n| Resource | Field | Meaning (reg-affairs truth) | Start (baseline, before difficulty) | Hard fail |\n|---|---|---|---|---|\n| **Capital ($M)** | `capital` | Cash runway. Everything costs money; you raise it at funding forks. | `$50M` | `<= 0` \u2192 LOSE (out of money) |\n| **Quarters** | `quartersLeft` | Time. Each turn = 1 fiscal quarter. The patent/investor clock. | `28` quarters (7 yr) | `<= 0` \u2192 LOSE (clock expired) |\n| **Evidence (0\u2013100)** | `evidence` | Strength of the clinical/nonclinical data package for the *current* stage. Resets-with-carryover at each stage gate. | `10` | n/a (low evidence \u2192 gate failures) |\n| **Goodwill (0\u2013100)** | `goodwill` | Regulatory relationship capital: pre-sub meetings attended, no shortcuts taken, clean conduct. FDA \"knows you and trusts your data.\" | `50` | `< 0` clamps to 0; very low \u2192 harsher review |\n\nPlus tracked scalars that aren't \"spent\" but drive logic & score:\n- `competitorProgress` (0\u2013100, advances each turn; reaching 100 = scooped).\n- `integrityFlags` (count of shortcuts taken \u2014 clinical holds, cut-corner CMC; each one taxes the final score and goodwill).\n- `dataQuality` (0\u2013100, a running weighted average of how well you've enrolled/powered each study \u2014 feeds the LLM reviewer and score).\n\n**Why these four (philosophical comment for the code):** *Drug development is the alchemy of turning money + time into belief. Capital and Quarters are what you spend; Evidence and Goodwill are what you must accumulate to buy the regulator's \"yes.\" The game is honest because, like real life, you can be cash-rich and still fail for lack of evidence \u2014 or data-perfect and still die because the runway ran out first.*\n\n---\n\n## 3. The Board \u2014 Regulatory Pathway as Stages\n\nThe board is a **linear stage track** (not the original valuation snake; that money-ladder is repurposed into the score/funding system). The track **diverges by intervention type** of the seeded trial. Each stage is a node with an entry effect, an action menu, and a **stage gate** (a checkpoint you must pass to advance). Crossing a gate consumes accumulated evidence and emits a per-stage score.\n\n### 3.1 DRUG / BIOLOGICAL pathway (`pathway: \"DRUG\"`)\nTriggered when lead intervention `type \u2208 {DRUG, BIOLOGICAL, GENETIC, DIETARY_SUPPLEMENT}` (BIOLOGICAL routes to a BLA at the end instead of NDA).\n\n| # | Stage `id` | Name | Entry / theme | Gate requirement |\n|---|---|---|---|---|\n| 0 | `PRE_IND` | Pre-IND | Nonclinical + CMC; optional Pre-IND meeting with FDA | evidence \u2265 25 |\n| 1 | `PHASE1` | Phase 1 (Safety) | Dose-finding, small N, MTD | evidence \u2265 35 |\n| 2 | `PHASE2` | Phase 2 (Proof of concept) | Efficacy signal, dose selection; optional End-of-Phase-2 meeting (big goodwill lever) | evidence \u2265 50 |\n| 3 | `PHASE3` | Phase 3 (Pivotal) | Large, powered, the expensive one | evidence \u2265 70 |\n| 4 | `NDA_BLA` | NDA / BLA submission | The marketing application \u2014 **LLM Reviewer milestone** (Section 7) | review pass |\n| 5 | `APPROVED` | Approval | Win state | \u2014 |\n\n### 3.2 DEVICE pathway (`pathway: \"DEVICE\"`)\nTriggered when lead intervention `type \u2208 {DEVICE, DIAGNOSTIC_TEST}`. **Sub-route by `device_class_hint` / `product_category`:** Class III or PMA-signal products take the **PMA** branch (pivotal required); everything else takes the **510(k)** branch (predicate + pilot, no full pivotal).\n\n| # | Stage `id` | Name | Entry / theme | Gate requirement |\n|---|---|---|---|---|\n| 0 | `PRE_SUB` | Pre-Submission (Q-Sub) | Bench + design; Pre-Sub meeting is the device-world goodwill superweapon | evidence \u2265 25 |\n| 1 | `PILOT` | Pilot / Feasibility study | Small first-in-human | evidence \u2265 40 |\n| 2 | `PIVOTAL` | Pivotal study | **PMA branch only:** required, expensive. **510(k) branch:** auto-skipped (advance with evidence \u2265 50 via predicate equivalence) | PMA: evidence \u2265 70 \u00b7 510(k): evidence \u2265 50 |\n| 3 | `SUBMISSION` | 510(k) or PMA submission | Marketing application \u2014 **LLM Reviewer milestone** | review pass |\n| 4 | `CLEARED` | Clearance / Approval | Win state | \u2014 |\n\n> **Divergence rule (must implement):** the seed endpoint computes `pathway` and `submissionType` (`NDA` | `BLA` | `510k` | `PMA`) from the lead intervention and class hint. The two pathways differ in stage count, gate thresholds, and per-stage costs (device pilot/pivotal are cheaper per-quarter than drug Phase 3 but 510(k) has a much weaker evidence ceiling \u2014 teaching the real \"least burdensome\" device contrast).\n\n### 3.3 COMBINATION / OBSERVATIONAL / ambiguous\n- `COMBINATION_PRODUCT` \u2192 DRUG pathway + a flavor flag `combo: true` that adds one extra \"assign the lead center (CDER vs CDRH)\" event (teaches the combination-product jurisdiction joke).\n- If the seeded trial is `OBSERVATIONAL` or has no clean intervention type, the seed endpoint **re-rolls** to a different interventional trial (the game needs a regulated product to shepherd). Fail loud server-side if no interventional trial is found after N tries.\n\n---\n\n## 4. Turn Loop & Action Set\n\nA turn = **one fiscal quarter**. The loop is strictly ordered so the engine is deterministic:\n\n```\nSTART TURN\n 1. UPKEEP   \u2014 quartersLeft -= 1; pay stage burn-rate (capital -= stageBurn);\n               competitorProgress += competitorSpeed (modified by your tempo);\n               if capital<=0 or quartersLeft<=0 or competitorProgress>=100 -> resolve LOSE.\n 2. ACTIONS  \u2014 player chooses 1..N actions until ALL action points (AP) spent.\n               Default AP per turn = 2. Actions cost AP + resources (table 4.1).\n 3. EVENT    \u2014 draw 1 event card from the active deck (Section 5), apply effects,\n               show log. (Some actions modify the draw, e.g. buy \"regulatory intel\".)\n 4. ROLL     \u2014 for each action this turn flagged `rolls:true` (mainly RUN_STUDY),\n               resolve its dice outcome now (Section 6) and apply evidence/quality deltas.\n 5. GATE?    \u2014 if player chose ADVANCE_STAGE and gate requirement met, cross gate:\n               emit stageScore, carry over (evidence *= 0.6), enter next stage.\n 6. END TURN \u2014 recompute dataQuality; autosave state; render.\n```\n\n### 4.1 Action set (the strategic verbs)\n\nEach action is `{type, payload}` (full contract in Section 10). `AP` = action-point cost.\n\n| Action `type` | AP | Cost | Effect | Reg-affairs lesson |\n|---|---|---|---|---|\n| `RUN_STUDY` | 2 | `$` scaled by stage + chosen rigor | Rolls dice (Sec 6); converts capital\u2192evidence & dataQuality. Player picks **rigor**: `lean` (cheap, low N, high variance) / `standard` / `gold` (expensive, high N, low variance, +goodwill). | Powering studies: you pay for certainty. |\n| `PRESUB_MEETING` | 1 | `$3M` + 1 quarter | +12 goodwill, reveals the next gate's true threshold, reduces next review's deficiency odds. Once per stage. | Talk to FDA *early*; meetings de-risk. |\n| `HIRE_CRO` | 1 | `$8M` upfront | +0.5 AP/turn for 4 turns (parallelize), small dataQuality boost. (The board's \"Hire CRO?\" fork.) | Outsourcing buys speed at cash cost. |\n| `RAISE_CAPITAL` | 1 | costs goodwill if done too often | +Capital (amount scaled by current evidence & valuation; high evidence = better terms). Dilution penalty to final score. | Fundraising on data strength; dilution is real. |\n| `STRENGTHEN_CMC` | 1 | `$4M` | +evidence (manufacturing/quality), reduces a class of review deficiencies. | CMC kills more NDAs than efficacy does. |\n| `BUY_INTEL` | 1 | `$2M` | Peek next 2 events; slow competitor +nothing (just info). | Competitive/regulatory intelligence. |\n| `TAKE_SHORTCUT` | 1 | `$0` | Big immediate evidence/time gain BUT +1 integrityFlag, \u221215 goodwill, raises clinical-hold event odds. | The tempting, score-taxing path. |\n| `ADVANCE_STAGE` | 1 | free | Attempt the stage gate this turn (resolved in step 5). | Knowing when you have \"enough.\" |\n| `SUBMIT_APPLICATION` | 2 | filing fee `$3M` | Only at `NDA_BLA`/`SUBMISSION` stage. Triggers the **LLM Reviewer** (Sec 7). | The big swing. |\n\n**Skill vs. luck:** the *choices* (when to advance vs. gather more evidence, rigor level, when to meet FDA, whether to shortcut, when to raise) are pure skill. Dice add variance *within* a chosen rigor band \u2014 higher rigor narrows the variance, so skilled play converts luck into near-certainty at a cash cost. This is the core teachable tension.\n\n### 4.6 Advisor personas (the 6 original characters \u2192 strategic modifiers)\nPick one at game start. Event System\n\nAt step 3 each turn, draw one event from the **active deck** chosen by weighted-random (seeded PRNG). Two decks mirror the board art:\n\n- **REG deck** \u2014 regulatory friction/fortune (drawn ~70% of turns).\n- **STAFF deck** \u2014 team/operational (drawn ~30%; the board's \"Draw new staff\").\n\nEach event: `{id, deck, title, flavor, category, weight, condition?, effects[]}`. `effects` are typed deltas applied through the engine (`{target: \"capital\"|\"evidence\"|\"goodwill\"|\"quartersLeft\"|\"competitorProgress\"|\"dataQuality\", op:\"add\"|\"mul\"|\"set\", value}`), optionally a `choice` (player picks A/B \u2014 a mini decision fork like the board's \"Get acquired? Sell!/No thanks!\").\n\n**Categories (REG):**\n1. `CLINICAL` \u2014 enrollment slow (\u2212evidence gain), site found, AE cluster (\u2212goodwill or clinical hold if integrityFlags high).\n2. `REGULATORY` \u2014 Refuse-to-File risk, advisory-committee scheduled, breakthrough/fast-track designation granted (skip-ahead bonus if evidence high), guidance changes mid-stream.\n3. `CMC_QUALITY` \u2014 manufacturing deviation, supplier issue (\u2212evidence unless STRENGTHEN_CMC done).\n4. `MARKET` \u2014 competitor stumbles (\u2212competitorProgress), payer skepticism, **acquisition offer** (a choice fork: take buyout = instant cash-out + a \"partial win\" score, or decline and keep climbing \u2014 the board's \"Sell!/No thanks!\").\n5. `FORTUNE` \u2014 grant funding (+capital, the board's \"You got the grant funding!\"), corporate venture round (the board's \"Corporate venture funds awarded!\").\n\n**Categories (STAFF):** key hire (+AP or +dataQuality), key departure (\u2212AP next turn), whistleblower (if integrityFlags > 0: big goodwill/score hit \u2014 *honesty enforced by the engine*), burnout (\u2212AP unless capital spent).\n\n**Determinism rule:** event selection uses `seededRandom`; weights are static data in `events.json`. Conditions (`integrityFlags > 0`, current stage, etc.) gate which events are eligible, then weighted-pick among eligible. ~40\u201360 cards at MVP, expandable as static JSON (the legacy \"32 cards\" is the comedic floor, not a cap).\n\n---\n\n## 6. Randomness & Dice (the two original dice, reimagined)\n\nThe board's two custom dice become the **RUN_STUDY resolver**, preserving the canon faces:\n\n- **Organ die** (BRAIN, LUNG, LIVER, PANCREAS, BONE, HEART) \u2192 seeds a **therapeutic-area flavor event** for the study and a tiny modifier if it matches the seeded trial's `therapeutic_area` (your team \"knows this organ\"): a matching roll gives a small evidence bonus. Mostly flavor + teaching that indication matters.\n- **Outcome die** (\u2713 \u2717 ! @, weighted) \u2192 the **study result**:\n  - \u2713 **Hit** \u2192 full evidence gain for the chosen rigor.\n  - @ **Mixed/Redo** \u2192 partial evidence, optional pay-to-rerun.\n  - ! **Complication** \u2192 small evidence + a CLINICAL event injected (AE, protocol deviation).\n  - \u2717 **Miss** \u2192 minimal evidence, dataQuality drop.\n\n**Rigor reshapes the die** (skill > luck): `lean` weights \u2717/@ heavier (cheap gamble); `gold` weights \u2713 heavily (you bought your certainty); `standard` is balanced. Implementation: each rigor is a probability vector over the 4 outcome faces, fed to `seededRandom`. Evidence delta = `baseGain[stage] * rigorMultiplier * outcomeMultiplier`, then nudged by goodwill and the organ-match bonus.\n\nAll dice resolve through one function `rollStudy(state, rigor) -> {organ, outcome, evidenceDelta, qualityDelta, injectedEvent?}` using the seeded PRNG. **Same seed + same actions = same game**, which is what makes server-side score verification possible.\n\n---\n\n## 7. The LLM \"FDA Reviewer\" (optional, with scripted fallback)\n\nTriggered by `SUBMIT_APPLICATION` at the submission stage. The player **composes or selects a short submission rationale** \u2014 a 1\u20133 sentence \"cover letter / benefit-risk statement,\" either free-typed or chosen from 3\u20134 canned options (canned options guarantee playability and seed the comedy).\n\n### 7.1 Endpoint: `POST /api/game/review`\n**Request (engine sends a compact, PII-free packet):**\n```json\n{\n  \"submissionType\": \"NDA\",                 // NDA|BLA|510k|PMA\n  \"indication\": \"Type 2 Diabetes\",         // from seeded trial therapeutic_area/condition\n  \"interventionType\": \"DRUG\",\n  \"rationaleText\": \"Our Phase 3 met its primary endpoint with a clean safety profile; benefit-risk is favorable.\",\n  \"metrics\": {                             // derived from final state, normalized 0-100\n    \"evidence\": 78, \"dataQuality\": 71, \"goodwill\": 64,\n    \"integrityFlags\": 1, \"presubMeetingsHeld\": 2,\n    \"enrollmentAdequacy\": 0.82             // (achieved vs needed N), see Sec 8\n  }\n}\n```\n\n**Response (strict, typed \u2014 the engine never trusts free text back):**\n```json\n{\n  \"decision\": \"APPROVABLE_WITH_DEFICIENCIES\", // APPROVED | APPROVABLE_WITH_DEFICIENCIES | COMPLETE_RESPONSE_LETTER | REFUSE_TO_FILE\n  \"scoreModifier\": -8,                         // integer, clamped [-25, +20]\n  \"deficiencies\": [\n    {\"code\": \"CMC\", \"severity\": \"minor\", \"text\": \"Stability data insufficient for 24-month shelf life.\"},\n    {\"code\": \"EFFICACY\", \"severity\": \"major\", \"text\": \"Single pivotal trial; consider confirmatory evidence.\"}\n  ],\n  \"reviewerQuip\": \"Reviewer note: We appreciate enthusiasm. We appreciate data more.\", // the funny line\n  \"fixCostQuarters\": 2,                         // if not APPROVED, quarters to address & resubmit\n  \"fixCostCapital\": 6                           // $M to address\n}\n```\n\n- **Model:** uses `settings.openai_model` (`gpt-4o-mini`) via `settings.openai_api_key`; or the DGX local Qwen / `runpod_endpoint_url` if configured. **System prompt** casts it as a deadpan, fair-but-exacting FDA reviewer who scores against the metrics, *must* return the strict JSON schema (use JSON mode / function-calling), and whose `reviewerQuip` is the satirical payload. Temperature low for fairness.\n- **Mapping rationale \u2192 outcome:** the LLM weighs `rationaleText` quality *lightly* and `metrics` *heavily* (so a slick sentence can't rescue a thin package \u2014 teaching that **data > narrative**, the whole joke of D. Lay, JD). Rubric handed to the model: evidence/dataQuality drive `decision`; `integrityFlags` and low goodwill push toward CRL/RTF; honest gaps acknowledged in the rationale earn small goodwill credit.\n\n### 7.2 Scripted fallback (game fully playable with no LLM)\n`POST /api/game/review` with `useLLM=false`, or any LLM error/empty key \u2192 deterministic rubric in code:\n```\nscore = 0.45*evidence + 0.30*dataQuality + 0.15*goodwill\n        + 12*(enrollmentAdequacy-0.5) - 10*integrityFlags + 4*presubMeetingsHeld\ndecision: score>=80 APPROVED \u00b7 60-79 APPROVABLE_WITH_DEFICIENCIES\n          \u00b7 40-59 COMPLETE_RESPONSE_LETTER \u00b7 <40 REFUSE_TO_FILE\ndeficiencies: pick from a static library keyed by which metric is weakest.\nreviewerQuip: pick from a static deadpan-quip array (seeded).\n```\nThe fallback emits the **same response schema**, so the engine is identical either way. **Fail-loud rule:** the engine calls review through one adapter; if the network call throws, log loud and transparently fall back \u2014 never silently swallow, never block the game.\n\n### 7.3 Resolving the review in the engine\n- `APPROVED` \u2192 cross to `APPROVED/CLEARED`, **WIN**, apply `scoreModifier`.\n- Otherwise \u2192 spend `fixCostQuarters` + `fixCostCapital`, address deficiencies (a guided mini-loop: each deficiency is a small action to clear), then `SUBMIT_APPLICATION` again. RTF/CRL repeated without enough evidence is how a cash-rich-but-data-poor player still loses.\n\n---\n\n## 8. Win / Lose & Scoring\n\n### 8.1 Outcomes\n- **WIN** \u2014 reached `APPROVED`/`CLEARED`.\n- **PARTIAL WIN** \u2014 accepted an acquisition buyout mid-game (capped score, flavored \"exit before approval\").\n- **LOSE** \u2014 capital \u2264 0, quartersLeft \u2264 0, or competitor reached 100.\n\n### 8.2 Leaderboard score (single integer, higher = better)\nA real reg pro should read this formula and nod. Computed **server-side** by replaying the log:\n\n```\nBASE              = 1000  (win) | 400 (partial win) | 0 (loss, but still scored for the board)\n\nSPEED_BONUS       = 25 * quartersLeft            // finishing early = patent life + investor joy\nCAPITAL_EFFICIENCY= 6  * capitalRemaining($M)     // didn't torch the runway\nEVIDENCE_QUALITY  = 5  * dataQuality              // strong, well-powered package\nGOODWILL_BONUS    = 3  * goodwill                 // good regulatory citizenship\nREVIEW_MODIFIER   = scoreModifier (from Sec 7)    // the reviewer's verdict, [-25..+20]\n\nINTEGRITY_PENALTY = -150 * integrityFlags         // shortcuts cost you, hard\nDILUTION_PENALTY  = -10  * capitalRaisedRounds     // over-fundraising taxes the exit\nWASTE_PENALTY     = -2   * wastedEvidenceOverGate  // evidence hoarded far past the gate = dithering\n\nDIFFICULTY_MULT   = seed.difficultyMultiplier (Sec 9, ~0.8\u20131.6)\n\nFINAL = round( (BASE + SPEED_BONUS + CAPITAL_EFFICIENCY + EVIDENCE_QUALITY\n               + GOODWILL_BONUS + REVIEW_MODIFIER + INTEGRITY_PENALTY\n               + DILUTION_PENALTY + WASTE_PENALTY) * DIFFICULTY_MULT )\nclamp FINAL >= 0\n```\n\n**Reads as:** *win fast, with cash left, on strong honest data, having been a good regulatory citizen.* Speed + efficiency + evidence quality rewarded; wasted resources and shortcuts punished \u2014 exactly the brief. Difficulty multiplier means clearing a hard real trial (PMA Class III oncology) outscores an easy one.\n\n### 8.3 Leaderboard table (`game_scores`, SQLite, reuses `app.db`)\n`id PK \u00b7 display_name TEXT \u00b7 email TEXT \u00b7 score INT \u00b7 seed_nct_id TEXT \u00b7 pathway TEXT \u00b7 outcome TEXT \u00b7 advisor TEXT \u00b7 created_at TIMESTAMP \u00b7 action_log_hash TEXT`. No auth (per brief); email used only to dedupe a player's best and for the existing Buttondown opt-in. Validate/escape inputs; the score is the *replayed* value, never the client's claim.\n\n---\n\n## 9. Difficulty From Real Data\n\nThe seed endpoint reads the chosen trial's real fields and derives starting parameters. This is what makes every game *factual* and re-teaches the data each round.\n\n| Real field | Drives | Rule |\n|---|---|---|\n| `intervention.type` (+`device_class_hint`, `product_category`) | **pathway / submissionType** | DRUG vs DEVICE branch; Class III/PMA-signal \u2192 PMA (hard); else 510(k) (easier). Section 3. |\n| `phase` | **starting stage + remaining distance** | Seeded trial already at `PHASE2` \u2192 you start at `PHASE2` with carried evidence; less track left = lower base difficulty but you inherit its baggage. `NA`/early \u2192 start `PRE_IND`. |\n| `therapeutic_area` | **organ-die match + indication flavor + difficulty** | Oncology/Neurology = higher `difficultyMultiplier` (hard endpoints, big trials); Dermatology/Other = lower. Also sets the organ-match bonus target. |\n| `enrollment_count` | **evidence cost & `enrollmentAdequacy`** | Bigger N target \u2192 each evidence point costs more capital/quarters (powering a 3000-pt trial is brutal); `enrollmentAdequacy` (Sec 7) = achieved vs this target. |\n| `overall_status` | **flavor + competitor speed** | `TERMINATED` seed \u2192 competitor starts ahead (\"this molecule has a graveyard\"); `RECRUITING` \u2192 neutral; `COMPLETED` \u2192 slight goodwill (\"known quantity\"). |\n| `lead_sponsor_class` | **starting capital** | `INDUSTRY` \u2192 +capital (deep pockets); `NIH`/`ACADEMIC`/`OTHER` \u2192 \u2212capital +goodwill (grant-funded underdog). Pairs with Prof. Goetta Grant. |\n| `approx_enrollment_rate_per_month` | **clinical-event odds** | Low rate \u2192 higher \"enrollment slow\" event weight (teaching enrollment is the silent trial-killer; nods to TrialCat's whole reason for existing). |\n\n```\ndifficultyMultiplier = clamp(\n   1.0\n   + areaWeight[therapeutic_area]          //  -0.2 .. +0.4\n   + (submissionType==\"PMA\" ? 0.3 : submissionType==\"510k\" ? -0.2 : 0)\n   + log10(max(enrollment_count,10))/20    //  bigger trials harder\n   - (phaseAlreadyAdvanced * 0.05),        //  starting later = a touch easier\n   0.8, 1.6)\n```\n\nStarting resources = baselines (Sec 2) adjusted by `lead_sponsor_class` and advisor (Sec 4.6). The seed endpoint **labels enrollment rate \"approximate\"** in any UI copy (the model's docstring is emphatic that reg pros spot sloppy math instantly).\n\n### 9.1 GameSeed object (returned by `GET /api/game/seed`)\n```json\n{\n  \"seed\": 1734300000,                 // int \u2192 PRNG seed; also lets you share/replay a game\n  \"trial\": {                          // straight from the trials table, PII-free, public CT.gov data\n    \"nctId\": \"NCT04280705\",\n    \"briefTitle\": \"...\",\n    \"phase\": \"PHASE2\",\n    \"studyType\": \"INTERVENTIONAL\",\n    \"therapeuticArea\": \"Oncology\",\n    \"leadSponsorClass\": \"INDUSTRY\",\n    \"enrollmentCount\": 420,\n    \"approxEnrollmentRatePerMonth\": 12.3,\n    \"leadIntervention\": {\"type\": \"DRUG\", \"name\": \"...\", \"deviceClassHint\": null, \"productCategory\": null}\n  },\n  \"pathway\": \"DRUG\",\n  \"submissionType\": \"NDA\",\n  \"startStage\": \"PHASE2\",\n  \"difficultyMultiplier\": 1.25,\n  \"startResources\": {\"capital\": 60, \"quartersLeft\": 28, \"evidence\": 22, \"goodwill\": 45},\n  \"competitorSpeed\": 4.0,             // competitorProgress per turn\n  \"organMatchTarget\": \"PANCREAS\",     // organ-die face that grants the bonus this game\n  \"ctgovUrl\": \"https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04280705\"\n}\n```\nSeed selection: weighted-random over `trials WHERE study_type='INTERVENTIONAL' AND phase IS NOT NULL AND interventions exist`, using the same PRNG family; re-roll on OBSERVATIONAL/no-intervention.
