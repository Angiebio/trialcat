2. Data Accuracy and Limitations

All data is sourced from the U.S. National Library of Medicine's ClinicalTrials.gov registry, a public-domain database maintained by the National Institutes of Health (NIH). trialcat does not independently verify the accuracy, completeness, or timeliness of the underlying data. Known limitations include:

  • Enrollment rates are approximate. They are computed from protocol start dates and primary completion dates, which overestimate the enrollment duration for trials with long primary endpoints. These figures should be treated as rough benchmarks, not precise measurements.
  • Therapeutic area classification is heuristic. It is derived from MeSH term hierarchy mapping and may not match the sponsor's intended classification.
  • Device class information is not authoritative. When present, it is heuristically extracted from free-text descriptions. Authoritative device classification data is maintained by the U.S. FDA.
  • Data may be stale. ClinicalTrials.gov records are updated by sponsors on varying schedules. trialcat refreshes its database periodically but does not guarantee real-time accuracy.
